How it works

Ksham is small on purpose. Underneath the timer there's really just a practice: name one thing, stay with it, be honest about it, and come back gently when you drift. Here's how that works — and what each step is quietly about.

1

Name the one thing

Type the single task you'll do right now. You can't start a session until you've named it. Then choose a length — 5, 25, 45, or 60 minutes.

Honesty. Focus starts with being clear about what you're actually here to do — not a vague "be productive."
2

Start the focus

Press Start Focus and the timer begins counting down. Your goal stays pinned on screen, so you always know what "focus" means right now.

Respect. Your time matters, and the goal on screen is the promise you just made to yourself.
3

Stay on the tab

If you drift away, Ksham notices the one thing it honestly can — that you've left — and rings to call you back: the screen turns red, an alarm loops, and the state reads "CRISIS — FIX THE ENGINES." It never blocks the other tab or traps you. It just refuses to let you wander off without noticing.

A bell, not a cage. Ksham can't police you, and doesn't try to. It only asks you to come back.
4

Return

Come back to the tab and everything settles — the screen flashes green, the alarm stops, and the countdown picks up exactly where it left off. There's no penalty for having left.

Forgiveness. Drifting is human. Returning is the whole point — it's what the name means.
5

Answer honestly

When the timer hits zero, Ksham asks one question: "Did you actually do it?" Nothing verifies your answer. Say yes and your streak grows, with a burst of confetti. Say no and it resets — no shame, Ksham just tells you it's okay to try again.

Honesty & responsibility. The truth is yours alone to tell. That's exactly where it counts.
6

Build the streak

Every honest yes adds a session to your streak of honest focus. A session in progress is saved automatically, so if you close the tab or refresh, Ksham restores the timer — right where you left it.

The practice compounds. Do the loop enough times and you're not just focusing — you're becoming someone who keeps their word to themselves.
Four ideas hold it all together — honesty, responsibility, respect, and forgiveness. Everything else is just a timer.
